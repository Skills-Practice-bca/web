Mahatma Gandhi

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is widely regarded as a global symbol of non-violence and peaceful resistance. Gandhi's teachings of truth, peace, and non-violent civil disobedience inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Key Contributions

  • Led India to independence from British rule through non-violent resistance.
  • Promoted the ideas of Swaraj (self-rule) and non-cooperation with colonial powers.
  • Inspired global movements for civil rights and freedom.
  • Emphasized the importance of truth (Satyagraha) and non-violence (Ahimsa).
